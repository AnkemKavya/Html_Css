(AFP) Four more men trapped in a semi-submerged Laos cave for 10 days were rescued on Saturday after one was successfully brought to the surface a day earlier, but two remained missing. They are among seven villagers who entered the narrow tunnels in search for gold but were cut off inside the cave in the remote mountain area of central Xaysomboun province after flash floods hit on 20 May, BBC reported. Lee Kian Lie, a Malaysian rescue diver, told AFP via Messenger that the four were freed after water was pumped out of the cavern. Also Read | World News Today Live Updates on May 30, 2026 : Four more men freed from flooded Laos cave after 10 days; five rescued so far, two still missing "Important they already out," Lee said. He declined to speculate on the condition of the two who remained unaccounted for. A video he took from the scene showed teams under a makeshift tent attending to four people lying on stretchers and covered with thermal blankets. The Thailand Rescue Diver Facebook page also posted that "rescue officials were able to bring out four more people trapped" at about 3:10 pm (0810 GMT) on Saturday. "A total of five people have now been rescued, while two remain missing," it said. What exactly had happened Seven men had been trapped in the cavern in a remote mountainous area of central Xaysomboun province since May 20, when flash floods stranded them as they searched for gold, early state media reports said. They were trapped after heavy rains triggered flash flooding that blocked the exit of the cavern. Five of the men were located alive on Wednesday, huddling in a narrow shaft around 300 metres (984 feet) from the cave mouth. Rescuers guided a bedraggled and muddy man over unsteady ground to safety on Friday.
